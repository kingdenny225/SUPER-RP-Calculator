Predicted BCR-free survival · 0–36 months
Research tool — not for clinical decision-making. This calculator reproduces a single-centre model with internal (bootstrap) validation only and no external validation. It applies only to prostate-cancer patients undergoing radical prostatectomy after neoadjuvant ADT. Estimates are probabilities for a group of similar patients, not certainties for one person, and become less precise beyond 24 months (few patients remained at risk by 36 months). Do not use it to guide treatment.
▶ How the estimate is calculated

The model is a Cox proportional-hazards model fitted to 241 patients (79 BCR events). The predicted survival is S(t) = exp(−H₀(t) · exp(LP)), where the linear predictor is the weighted sum below and H₀(t) is the Breslow baseline cumulative hazard.

Baseline cumulative hazard: H₀(24)=0.0203, H₀(36)=0.0274. Risk tiers use fixed cut-points on the predicted 3-year BCR-free probability — Low > 81.5%, Intermediate 63.5–81.5%, High < 63.5% — which correspond to tertiles of predicted risk in the development cohort. The observed 3-year BCR-free rates in those tertiles were ~89% (low), ~68% (intermediate), and ~29% (high).

Model discrimination: optimism-corrected C-index 0.758; time-dependent AUC 0.80 (2 yr) and 0.74 (3 yr); calibration slope 0.96. Development cohort: SUPER-PC-RP, March 2016 – February 2026.
